Result & Conclusion

Results

  • The Hysteresis (B-H) loop was successfully plotted on the CRO screen for different magnetic specimens.
  • The Hysteresis Loss per cycle was calculated from the area of the loop.

Conclusions

From the experimental observations, we can conclude that:

  1. Soft Iron has a narrow hysteresis loop, indicating low retentivity and coercivity. This implies low hysteresis loss, making it suitable for making temporary magnets and transformer cores.
  2. Steel has a broad hysteresis loop with high retentivity and coercivity. This implies high hysteresis loss, making it suitable for permanent magnets but not for devices subjected to rapid AC cycles.
  3. Hysteresis loss is directly proportional to the frequency of the AC supply and the volume of the material.
